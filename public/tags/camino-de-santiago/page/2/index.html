8: Gone

Posted on December 20, 2017 in Camino de Santiago

I’ve sunken into the depths of an all-too-familiar melancholy. She is gone. I am alone. My emotions swing to the erratic cadence of an unforgiving metronome. I’m fine. Tick. I’m fine. Tick. I’m not. Tick. I miss the jade of her eyes, those luminescent opals I so often lose myself in. The ease of her smile, always able to bring about my own. The way she plucked those juicy blackberries from their thorny brambles, always a tad out of reach, the hem of her shirt lifting ever so slightly in the process, revealing the contours of a titillating landscape I secretly wished to explore.

1: Beginnings

Posted on December 13, 2017 in Camino de Santiago

The Camino de Santiago is a network of pilgrimage routes that ultimately converge on the city of Santiago de Compostela in Northwestern Spain, where the great Saint James is rumored to be buried. The most popular route is the Camino Frances. It begins at Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port in Southern France, a small city located north of the Pyrenees, and totals about 500 miles across Spain to the city of Santiago de Compostela. Many walk the Camino for spiritual and religious reasons; I walked in search of myself.
